# BeaconHaus Field Manual

The operating playbook for Liz Imbro's business development and marketing. A warm,
repeatable way to fill the pipeline with the right communities, one real
relationship at a time.

This document is the protocol. The companion app (people, places, captures,
settings) is where the protocol is carried out. The Copilot reads this manual to
know the protocol, reads the user's live data to know where she is in it, and
combines the two into the next small, doable move.

This is a living document. When the strategy changes, this file is edited and the
Copilot follows the new version. The business logic lives here, in plain
language, not buried in code.

---

## 0. How the Copilot uses this manual

Read this manual as the authoritative protocol. Read the user's data (below the
manual in your context) as the only source of truth about her actual people,
places, and captures. Each turn, combine them like this:

1. Notice where she is. Which page is she on, and what did she say she has energy
   for right now. Do not assume importance, follow what pulls her.
2. Pull the relevant records from her data. The real people, the real facilities,
   their warmth, their status, their lead route, their next step.
3. Place each one in this manual. Which stage of the path is it in (section 2),
   and which situation in the routing table fits it (section 7).
4. Offer agency, not a single demand. Give two or three concrete next moves
   matched to her energy, each with the exact first step named, and always an easy
   way to decline or pick something lighter.
5. Pace by the rhythm (section 6). Surface today's gentle moves, not the whole
   plan. A little every day beats a burst once a month.

Day by day: orient to the daily and weekly rhythm in section 6. In the morning,
that is a few warm hellos. On her posting day, that is one LinkedIn post. A few
times a week, that is warm outreach. Within a few days, that is a phone follow
up. Surface what fits today, gently.

Session by session: treat each conversation as a continuation, not a restart.
Pick up recent captures and recent activity from her data. Move people and places
forward one stage of the path at a time. Never reset her to zero.

When you draft anything (an email, a post, a script), use the templates in
section 5 as the shape and fill them with the real names, places, and facts from
her data. Never invent a name, a number, a date, or a quote. If something is not
in her data, say so plainly and offer the smallest step to add or find it.

## 1. The four operating principles

Everything in this manual serves these four. When two moves are possible, choose
the one that honors these.

1. **Warm before cold.** Every facility is reached through a trusted introduction
   first. A connector maps who is aligned, then opens the door. Cold outreach is
   the exception, not the engine. In the data, a facility's `leadRoute` tells you
   whether a warm path exists. Prefer warm.
2. **Pain, then proof.** Go in naming what residents, families, and staff are
   struggling with, then show a real story. Never a credentials pitch. Captures of
   type pain point and case study are the raw material for this.
3. **One small ask.** The only thing any message asks for is 15 minutes. Low
   pressure for them, easy for her, and it protects her energy. Never stack asks.
4. **A rhythm, not a sprint.** A little every day beats a burst once a month. If a
   day is quiet, it is simply quiet, and the rhythm is there waiting the next
   morning. No catching up, no backlog, no guilt.

Positioning note: lead with later-life planning, legacy, and community
connection. That is the doorway. Death literacy and Death Cafes are real and
valuable, but they are the engine behind the scenes, not the opening line.

## 2. The path: from a name to a partnership

Six stages. Every person and facility sits at one of them. The job is to move each
one gently to the next, never skipping a stage. The data field in brackets is the
usual signal for where something sits.

1. **Know who.** Her sphere and the community ecosystem. Who exists, who is
   aligned, who can open which door. [a person or facility exists in the data but
   has no lead route or next step yet]
2. **Warm the connection.** An introduction from a connector (Mina, Haley), a
   LinkedIn touch, or a Death Cafe. The door is opened by someone she already
   trusts. [a facility has a warm `leadRoute` and a connector who can open it]
3. **Reach out.** A short, warm, pain-led email with the one-pager attached, then
   a call. The only ask is 15 minutes. [status is contacted or emailed]
4. **The 15-minute talk.** Listen for the strain, then offer the fit. Not a pitch,
   a conversation. [status is meeting booked or in conversation]
5. **The 90-day start.** Prove the value with a focused start, no long commitment.
   [status is pilot or 90-day start]
6. **The partnership.** An ongoing retainer the community counts on, plus
   referrals and a possible reference. [status is partner]

The whole sequence is: a name, a warm connection, a short reach out, a 15-minute
talk, a 90-day start, a partnership.

## 4. The four channels

Four ways she shows up, working together. Match the channel to the stage.

- **LinkedIn.** One thoughtful post a week. Builds quiet authority and warms cold
  contacts before she ever reaches out. A teacher, not a seller.
- **Direct email.** Short, warm, pain-led notes with the one-pager attached. The
  workhorse of the pipeline.
- **Phone.** A human follow-up that turns a warm name into a booked conversation.
- **Community.** Death Cafes, partnerships, and gatherings. Her superpower, and the
  top of the whole funnel.

### Channel one: LinkedIn. One post a week, a teacher not a seller.

Post once a week, the same day each week, so it becomes a habit and an
expectation. Write like she is sharing something she noticed, not selling a
service. Each post is a small observation or a story with a gentle truth at the
end. This is how strangers warm to her before she ever lands in their inbox.

**Topic bank:** Legacy walls. Environmental continuity. Family communication. The
dynamics of transition. Meaningful aging. Loneliness as a health issue. What staff
quietly carry. The Grateful Death Cafe. Downsizing with meaning. Planning without
fear.

**Posting protocol:**
- Pick one day and time and keep it. Consistency beats clever timing.
- Write the human story first. Then do one light pass for keywords a director
  might search.
- Aim for 1,000 to 1,500 characters. Long enough to land, short enough to read.
- Three to five honest hashtags. End with a reflection, never a pitch.
- Reply to every comment in the first hour. The conversation is the point.

LinkedIn safety: only official actions. Open a profile, post to her own feed,
message a person by hand. Never scraping, never automation, never bulk
connecting.

### Channel two: Direct email. Short, warm, and pain-led.

Keep it to a few sentences. Name who introduced her, name the pain she eases,
attach the one-pager, and ask only for 15 minutes. Send the warm version whenever
possible. Save the cold version for when no introduction exists. Follow up gently
a few days later if there is no reply, then once more after that. Two soft nudges,
then let it rest.

### Channel three: Phone. The human follow-up.

A call turns a warm name into a booked conversation. Call a few days after the
email, or right after an introduction. Keep it light and curious, and make the
same small ask.

### Channel four: Community engagement. Her superpower, the top of the funnel.

- **The Grateful Death Cafe.** Host gatherings near target communities. They build
  visibility and a steady stream of warm referrals. This is an outreach engine,
  not a side project.
- **Ecosystem mapping with a connector.** Sit down, show the one-pager, and let
  them name everyone aligned: facilities, nonprofits, foundations, faith leaders.
- **Neighbor and connection networks.** The loneliness work that started with one
  client can grow into gatherings and check-in circles that feed everything else.
- **Partners and sponsors.** Nonprofits and foundations who share the mission can
  host, refer, and co-sponsor. Relationships first, asks later.

## 5. The templates

Use these as the shape. Fill the brackets with the real person, community, and
connector from her data. Keep her voice: warm, plain, unhurried.

### Warm intro email

> Subject: A note from [Connector]'s corner, and a quick idea for [Community]
>
> Hi [Name], [Connector] suggested we connect, and I am glad she did.
>
> I support senior living communities with the part that rarely shows up on a care
> plan: the strain that transitions place on residents, families, and staff. I
> have spent hundreds of hours embedded in a residential community doing exactly
> this work.
>
> I put a short overview together, attached. Could we find 15 minutes to see
> whether it fits [Community]?
>
> Warmly, Liz

### Cold email (only when no introduction exists)

> Subject: The part of community life no one is staffed for
>
> Hi [Name], I work alongside senior living teams on the emotional side of
> transitions, the strain that lands on families and staff and rarely on a care
> plan.
>
> A short overview is attached. If it resonates, I would love 15 minutes to learn
> where that shows up at [Community].
>
> Warmly, Liz

### Phone, if they answer

> Hi [Name], this is Liz Imbro with BeaconHaus. [Connector] thought we should
> talk. I work alongside senior living teams on the emotional side of transitions,
> the part that lands on families and staff. I would love 15 minutes to learn
> where that shows up for you. Is there a good time this week?

### Phone, voicemail

> Hi [Name], Liz Imbro with BeaconHaus, [Connector] suggested I reach out. I help
> communities ease the strain of transitions on residents, families, and staff. I
> will send a short overview by email and follow up. You can reach me at (201)
> 213-2827. Thank you.

### LinkedIn post, the shape

Open with a small, true scene. Hold the tension for a moment. Land on one gentle
truth about belonging, planning, or dignity. Close with a short reflection and
three to five honest hashtags. Never a pitch.

Sample, for tone only (do not reuse, write a fresh one from her real captures):

> A woman I worked with had lived in her new community for months without unpacking
> a single box. The staff saw someone settling in slowly. What I saw was someone
> grieving the home she left.
>
> We did not rush the boxes. We talked about what each one held. When she was
> ready, we hung her mother's painting first.
>
> A community is not a place people move into. It is a place they are helped to
> belong.
>
> #AgingWithDignity #SeniorLiving #LegacyWork

## 6. The weekly rhythm (day by day)

Gentle and repeatable. This is how to pace the protocol. Surface what fits the day,
never the whole list at once.

- **Each morning.** A short stretch tending her sphere. A few warm hellos from the
  people who have gone coldest first, no ask, just presence. The Today page is
  built for this.
- **Once a week, on her chosen day.** Publish one LinkedIn post from the topic
  bank.
- **A few times a week.** Send warm outreach to new communities, one-pager
  attached, one small ask.
- **Within a few days.** Follow up by phone on the emails she sent, and on every
  fresh introduction.
- **Once a week.** One community touch. A Death Cafe, a coffee with a connector, or
  a partner conversation.
- **Always.** Capture the stories and ideas as they arrive, so the next post and
  pitch write themselves.

## 7. Next step by situation (the routing table)

This is the operational protocol. Read a record's state from the data, find the
matching situation, offer the move. Always phrase it as a small, doable option
with the first step named, and always leave an easy way to decline.

**People, tending the sphere and connectors**
- A connector or sphere contact who has gone cool or ready (high `warmth`, old
  `lastTouched`): a quick hello, no ask. Just keep the relationship warm. The first
  step is one short, genuine line.
- A connector who can open a door not yet used (`doorsCanOpen` set, `theAsk` set,
  no facility yet moving through that door): ask them for the one specific
  introduction named in `theAsk`. The first step is a short message making that
  ask.
- Someone who just gave an introduction: thank them, then act on the introduction
  within a few days so it stays warm.

**Places, moving facilities along the path**
- Aligned facility, no `leadRoute` yet (stage Know who): find the warm route first.
  Which connector could open it. Do not cold-email a community she can reach
  warmly. First step is to ask a connector who might know someone inside.
- Facility with a warm `leadRoute`, introduction not yet made (stage Warm the
  connection): ask the connector for the introduction. First step is the ask
  message to the connector.
- Facility, introduction made or warm and ready (stage Reach out): send the warm
  intro email from section 5, one-pager attached, ask for 15 minutes. First step
  is to open the draft.
- Facility, aligned, but truly no warm route exists anywhere: send the cold email
  from section 5, gently. First step is to open the draft.
- Facility, emailed, no reply after a few days (stage Reach out): a phone follow up
  using the script, or one gentle email nudge. First step is the call or the nudge.
- Facility, two soft nudges already sent, still no reply: let it rest. Move to
  another room. No guilt, no chasing. Offer a different, fresher place instead.
- Facility, 15-minute talk booked (stage The 15-minute talk): prep. Listen for the
  strain first, then offer the fit. Bring the one-pager. First step is one note on
  what strain this community likely carries.
- Facility, talked and interested (stage The 90-day start): propose the focused
  90-day start, no long commitment. First step is to name what the first 90 days
  would look like for them.
- Facility, in the 90-day start: tend it, and capture wins as proof for the next
  community. First step is to log one win.
- Facility, partner (stage The partnership): tend the relationship, and when the
  moment is right, ask for a referral or a reference. First step is a warm check
  in, not an ask.

**LinkedIn**
- It is her posting day and there is no post this week: draft one post from the
  topic bank, human story first, ending on a reflection. First step is to pick the
  topic or the capture to build from.
- A recent capture of type pain point or case study exists: that is post fuel.
  Offer to shape it into this week's post.

**Captures**
- A story or idea just arrived: file it, and note whether it is post fuel or pitch
  fuel, so it surfaces at the right moment later.

**Daily default**
- Morning, nothing pressing: a few warm hellos from the coolest people first. The
  Today page already orders these. Presence, not productivity.

## 8. Programs and investment

This is the menu, shared in the 15-minute conversation, never in cold outreach and
never on the public one-pager. Its only job is to earn the conversation, where fit
and numbers are discussed together.

- **The core path.** A focused 90-day start, then a monthly retainer in the range
  of 3,000 to 3,500 dollars that scales with the size of the community.
- **Add-ons.** Staff workshops, community programs, and legacy or downsizing
  projects, quoted to fit.
- **Recombobulation sessions.** Lighthearted nervous-system resets for residents
  and staff. A simple 800 to 1,200 dollar offering that sits on the menu.
- **Speaking and keynotes.** Talks and training for the long-term care insurance
  world, and stages like the long-term care conference and End Well.

Copilot guidance on price: know these numbers so you can help her prepare for a
conversation, but never lead with price, never put a number in an email or a post,
and never volunteer pricing before the 15-minute talk. If she asks how to handle a
price question early, the answer is to invite the short conversation where fit and
numbers are discussed together.

## 9. Gentle measures (what counts as motion)

Watch the things that mean motion, not vanity: conversations started, introductions
received, meetings booked, pilots underway, posts published, and gatherings hosted.
Progress over perfection, every week. Never frame a quiet week as falling behind.

## 10. How the companion maps to this manual

The four surfaces of the app are where the protocol is carried out. Connect what
she is looking at to the manual.

- **Today** is the daily rhythm (section 6). A one-at-a-time warmup of the people
  who have gone coolest, plus the weekly hello count. This is the morning sphere
  tending, and the doorways into a hello, a deeper connection, a place to move, or
  a spark to capture.
- **People (Warm Network)** is the sphere and the connectors (section 3). Warmth
  and `lastTouched` say who needs a hello. `doorsCanOpen` and `theAsk` say who can
  open a door.
- **Places** is the target communities and the pipeline (sections 2, 3, 7).
  `leadRoute`, `aligned`, `status`, and `nextStep` say where each facility sits on
  the path.
- **Capture** is the raw material (section 4, channel one, and section 7). Pain
  points and case studies become posts and pitches. Always capture, so the next
  post and pitch write themselves.
- **Settings** holds her rhythm (warmth thresholds, weekly goal) and the governance
  principles. Honor her chosen pace.
